ABOUT MY BACKGROUND AND EXPERIENCE

After experiencing a middle-class suburban upbringing punctuated by numerous years living in various countries in Asia, I studied psychology and philosophy, lived in France a couple of years, and eventually earned my doctorate in personality psychology at the University of Michigan in1982.  I also trained there in psychodynamic counseling and therapy. Since then, I have been a psychology professor, author, community organizer, and activist. I have played a part in the international movement to establish critical psychology, a perspective that challenges the individualism and scientism of mainstream psychology.  My books include:

​

  • Life Choices: Understanding Dilemmas and Decisions

  • Damaged Life: The Crisis of the Modern Psyche

  • Critical Psychology: Voices for Change

Most of my academic work has aimed to foster ideology critique from a left psychoanalytic point of view, with special emphasis on dialogue practices.  A major purpose of this is to understand and remove the psychosocial obstacles to the construction of humane communities.

My activism began with anti-nuclear and Central America solidarity work in the 1980s, Green Party organizing in the 1990s, and anti-war organizing post-9/11.  I then worked in Washington DC as the national co-coordinator of Psychologists for Social Responsibility for four years.  Since moving to Portland, Oregon in 2004, I have worked with local sustainability projects such as Tryon Life Community Farm and Transition Portland, as well as the Portland Central America Solidarity Committee.  In general, I am interested in accompanying community spaces in which we can reflect on our deepest yearnings and work collectively to alter social life accordingly.

Having been a visiting professor in Venezuela, Nicaragua, Mexico, and Costa Rica, I am fluent in Spanish and happy to work in a bilingual mode.

I am a founding member of the Cascadia Center for Social Ecology, which focuses on developing support and resources for activists.  More recently, I helped establish the Social Justice Action Center in Portland.
